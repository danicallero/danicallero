A year ago, I participated in HackUDC and learnt quite a bit. This year felt different. I decided to step behind the curtain and see how things actually get built.

Organizing an event for 500 people teaches you humility. You wear many hats — support, logistics, design, and despite the irony, you end up coding more than you do as a regular participant.

## What I built

In the last few months, I worked on:

- Contributing to the [official event website](https://hackudc.gpul.org)
- Building an operations app in React Native to manage logistics during the event
- Filing the paperwork to establish GPUL's Apple Developer account (which, surprisingly, required weeks of back-and-forth with various institutions)

The project thatm quite frankly, almost made me wanna quit was FastTrack — a system to manage evaluations for 120 projects across 12 rooms, all happening at once. I built it in three nights. By all architectural standards, it's rough code. But it worked. It handled 500 concurrent users without crashing, and that mattered more than anything.

## What actually matters

The part that stayed with me wasn't the code. It was the people.

When I tell people outside this circle about the late nights and the sheer volume of work I took on, some tell me I’m crazy. They look at the effort and the "price tag"—or lack thereof—and insist I shouldn't have done all of this for free.

Honestly? They just don’t get it.

They don't see the sense of achievement that comes from watching a system you built hold steady under pressure, or the deep sense of community found in a room full of tired, motivated people. You can’t put a price on the passion we had for this project.

Working alongside people who spent countless hours packing welcome kits, arranging catering plans, or solving logistical problems they didn't have to solve was one of the most rewarding experiences of my entire life.

I'm grateful I got to be part of this. The event happened. It was well-received. And I got to meet so many incredible people along the way.

If you want a glimpse of the event, you can check out the [official photo gallery](https://cloud.gpul.org/s/XyfQ2eGzLiJfRx4).

I can't wait to see what we build next year.
